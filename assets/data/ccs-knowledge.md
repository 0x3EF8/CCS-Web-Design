### 2.3 BSCS vs. BSIT â€” Complete Comparison

| Dimension | BSCS | BSIT |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | Theory + Research + Deep CS fundamentals | Practice + Industry + Applied IT solutions |
| **Mathematics level** | High (Discrete Math, Calculus, Statistics, Algorithm proofs) | Moderate (Basic Calculus, Statistics â€” less proof-heavy) |

| **Graduate school** | Excellent preparation for MS/PhD in CS | Possible, but less common |
| **Starting salary (PH)** | â‚±25,000â€“â‚±45,000/month (entry) | â‚±20,000â€“â‚±40,000/month (entry) |

| **Tracks available** | 4 tracks | 3 tracks |
| **CHED accredited** | Yes | Yes |

**Who should choose BSCS?**
- Students who enjoy mathematics, logic puzzles, and abstract thinking
- Those interested in AI, machine learning, or research careers
- Anyone planning to pursue graduate school or a PhD
- Those who want to understand HOW computers and systems work at a deep level

**Who should choose BSIT?**
- Students who want to build websites, apps, or manage IT systems quickly
- Those more interested in the "what" and "how" of technology rather than the "why"
- Practical learners who prefer coding projects over theory
- Anyone aiming for web development, IT administration, or cloud roles

**If you're truly unsure:** BSIT allows you to shift to BSCS within Year 1 (with chairperson approval). Start with BSIT if you're unsure â€” it's the safer choice for most students.

## SECTION 10 â€” COMPREHENSIVE FAQ

### Programs & Curriculum
**Q: What is the difference between BSCS and BSIT?**
A: BSCS = deeper computer science theory, algorithms, AI, systems research. BSIT = practical application, web/app/network development, enterprise IT. BSCS has a higher math requirement and is better for graduate school. BSIT is more immediately industry-ready.

**Q: Which program is harder?**
A: BSCS is generally considered more academically demanding due to heavy mathematics (discrete math, algorithm proofs, theory of computation). BSIT is still rigorous but more practical, with less abstract theory.

**Q: Can I shift from BSIT to BSCS or vice versa?**
A: Yes â€” shifting is allowed within Year 1, subject to chairperson approval and available slots. Early decision is better; shifting in Year 2 may result in units not being credited.

**Q: What programming languages will I learn?**
A: Both programs cover Python, Java, JavaScript, and SQL. BSCS adds C/C++, Haskell (theory), and assembly. BSIT adds PHP, React, Flutter/Dart, and cloud scripting.

**Q: Do I need to know programming before enrolling?**
A: No! Year 1 starts from the fundamentals. Prior exposure helps but is not required.

**Q: Is there a thesis / capstone project?**
A: Yes. Both BSCS and BSIT require a 2-semester capstone project in Year 4. BSCS students may pursue research-based or systems-development thesis. BSIT students typically build a working software system for an industry client or community need.

### Admissions

**Q: What are the minimum GPA requirements?**
A: For fresh SHS graduates, a general average applies (check current cutoff with admissions office). Transferees need minimum 2.5 GPA (80%). Contact the Registrar's for current cutoff scores.

**Q: When is the application deadline?**
A: Typically Juneâ€“July for 1st Semester. Check the Registrar's Office for the exact date each year. With only 3 slots remaining, apply now.

**Q: Is the SHS strand important?**
A: STEM strand is preferred for BSCS. TVL-ICT or STEM is preferred for BSIT. Other SHS strands are accepted but may require bridging/review before Year 1 math courses.

**Q: Can senior high school students apply early?**
A: Yes â€” conditional applications may be processed with Grade 11 cards. Final admission is confirmed upon SHS graduation and receipt of Form 138.

### Career & Certification

**Q: Is there a board exam for BSCS/BSIT?**
A: Both programs' graduates can take the **Civil Service Professional Examination**. BSIT graduates may also qualify for the **Electronics Engineering Board Exam** under certain conditions. Industry certifications strongly recommended: AWS, CISCO (CCNA), CompTIA A+/Security+, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure.

**Q: How long is the OJT / internship?**
A: **486 hours** for both programs â€” approximately 3 months at 40 hours/week. Typically completed in Year 4 (1st or 2nd semester). Students can choose their own OJT company subject to department approval.

**Q: What companies hire CCS graduates?**
A: Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Accenture, IBM, Grab, Sea Ltd., Concentrix, Sutherland, BPI, BDO, government agencies (DOST, NCC, DICT), startups, hospitals, and e-commerce companies.

**Q: What is the starting salary for graduates?**
A: BSCS fresh grads: â‚±25,000â€“â‚±45,000/month. BSIT fresh grads: â‚±20,000â€“â‚±40,000/month. Salary depends heavily on track, certifications, portfolio quality, and company. Metro Manila rates are generally 20â€“30% higher than provincial.

### Graduate Programs

**Q: Does CCS offer graduate programs (Masters, PhD)?**
A: The CCS Department focuses on undergraduate programs. The **Graduate School** offers MIT (Master of Information Technology) and MCS (Master of Computer Science). Inquire at the Registrar's Office or Graduate School.

## 10. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: What is the difference between BSCS and BSIT?**
A: BSCS is more theoretical and research-oriented (algorithms, AI, systems), while BSIT is more applied and industry-focused (web dev, networking, enterprise systems). Both lead to excellent tech careers.

**Q: Can I shift from BSIT to BSCS (or vice versa)?**
A: Yes, shifting is allowed within the first year with the department chairperson's approval and subject to slot availability.

**Q: What are the minimum entrance exam scores for admission?**
A: Specific cutoff scores depend on the current year's competition. Contact the admissions office for the latest figures.

**Q: Is there a board exam for BSCS/BSIT graduates?**
A: Yes â€” the Civil Service Examination and various industry certifications (AWS, CompTIA, CISCO) are available. BSIT graduates may take the Electronics Engineering Board Exam under certain conditions.

**Q: Does the CCS Department offer graduate programs?**

**Q: How long is the OJT / internship?**
A: 486 hours (approximately 3 months, 40 hours/week) for both BSCS and BSIT students, typically in 4th year.

**Q: Are there international exchange programs?**
A: The school has partner universities abroad. CCS students may apply through the International Affairs Office.

**Q: What companies hire CCS graduates?**
A: Major tech companies (local and multinational), BPO/IT outsourcing firms, government agencies (DOST, NCC, PhilSys), banks (for fintech), and startups. Named partners include Google, Microsoft, Accenture, IBM, and various local IT companies.
